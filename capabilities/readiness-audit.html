Why this audit exists

Most AI projects do not fail because the model is weak. They fail because the workflow around it is unclear.

Before building an AI system, teams need to know which workflow is ready, which data can be trusted, which tools need to connect, where human review belongs, and what outcome is worth measuring.

The audit gives teams a practical starting point. Not a generic AI strategy. Not a long innovation report. A clear view of what can be built now, what needs preparation, and what should not be automated yet.

What we assess

We audit the workflow around the AI.

The audit looks at the operational layer that determines whether AI will be useful in practice: the business goal, the workflow, the data, the systems, the review points, the risks, and the first measurable build.

01

Business outcome

What should improve: time saved, cost reduced, revenue lifted, quality improved, or risk reduced?

02

Workflow reality

How the work actually moves today: triggers, people, tools, reviews, handoffs, exceptions, and bottlenecks.

03

Data readiness

Where the information lives, who owns it, whether it is current, structured, searchable, complete, and safe to use.

04

Tool readiness

Which systems need to connect, whether APIs or exports are available, and what can be done without disrupting the stack.

05

Human review

Where AI can prepare, draft, classify, or route — and where people must approve, edit, or decide.

06

Risk and governance

Sensitive data, permissions, audit trail needs, DPIA considerations, EU AI Act relevance, and review responsibilities.

07

Quick-win path

The smallest useful workflow that can prove value quickly.

Readiness score

The result is a practical readiness score, not a vague recommendation.

Each workflow is scored across the audit lenses so the next step is clear. Some workflows are ready to build. Some need better data, ownership, or review paths first. Some should not be automated yet.
