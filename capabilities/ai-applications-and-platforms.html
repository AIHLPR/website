Scaling journey

Start with one workflow. Grow into an operating layer.

The safest way to build internal AI is not to automate everything at once. Start with one high-friction workflow, prove the value, then expand into the connected places where the same context, approvals, and audit trail can create more leverage.

01 Stage Start with one team Pick a workflow where work already gets stuck: an inbox, approval queue, document review, product request, or support handover. Business outcome: A focused first version that saves time without changing how the whole company works.
02 Stage Expand the workflow Once the first workflow is trusted, connect the next step: routing, review, notifications, exports, or reporting. Business outcome: Less manual coordination between teams and fewer dropped tasks.
03 Stage Connect the systems Bring in the systems your team already uses: CRM, ERP, document storage, spreadsheets, ticketing tools, or internal databases. Business outcome: People stop hunting across tools before making a decision.
04 Stage Operate with visibility Add permissions, review states, audit logs, usage tracking, and improvement loops. Business outcome: Leadership gets a system that can be trusted, monitored, and improved over time.

Where it lives

Engineered into your stack.

Clear system boundaries matter. Deployment, identity, integrations, model choice, and data controls are defined before the workflow goes live.

01

Deployment

Cloud, private network, or on-prem deployment. Region-pinned where required, with customer-held keys on request.

  • AWS · Azure · GCP
  • Private network / on-prem
  • Region pinning · EU residency
  • VPC isolation
  • BYO key management (KMS)
  • Sovereign deployment
02

Identity

Existing SSO and identity provider. Roles, scopes, and access decisions stay within the organisation’s control.

  • SAML · OIDC · SCIM
  • Roles inherit from your IDP
  • MFA enforced by you
  • Just-in-time access
  • Per-tenant role scoping
03

Integrations

Connects to existing business systems. No rip-and-replace. No parallel operating layer.

  • Salesforce · HubSpot
  • SAP · NetSuite · Oracle
  • M365 · Google Workspace
  • SharePoint · Confluence
  • Snowflake · Databricks · Postgres
  • Kafka · Service Bus · webhooks
04

Model layer

The right model for the task. Swappable as the landscape changes, with routing for cost, latency, and control.

  • GPT · Claude · Gemini
  • Open weights (Llama, Mistral)
  • Fine-tuned on your domain
  • LLM router · cost/latency aware
  • No vendor lock-in
05

Data sovereignty

Data stays inside defined boundaries. Every action is recorded, scoped, and reviewable.

  • Encryption at rest & in transit
  • Scoped reads · scoped writes
  • Immutable action log
  • Per-tenant isolation
  • GDPR / SOC 2 / ISO 27001 ready
Cloud-agnostic. AWS · Azure · GCP · on-prem Your perimeter. data never leaves your boundary Model-agnostic. swap models as the landscape shifts IT-reviewed. deployable as documented
Defensible by design

Not just an AI answer. A decision your team can defend.

Internal AI becomes useful when the answer is explainable, reviewable, and connected to the way the business already works. The system should not simply generate a response. It should show the context, apply the rules, route the decision, and record what happened.

Request in
01Input layer

The messy inputs are handled first.

Documents, emails, forms, records, and notes are cleaned and structured before AI is asked to do useful work.

What this means for the business

The system is less likely to break when inputs are inconsistent.

02Context layer

The right context is assembled.

The application pulls together the relevant sources, history, and rules before producing a recommendation or draft.

What this means for the business

Teams spend less time searching and more time deciding.

03Workflow layer

The workflow decides what happens next.

The system can draft, route, escalate, notify, export, or pause for review depending on the business rules.

What this means for the business

AI becomes part of the workflow, not a separate chat window.

04Control layer

Controls stay visible.

Permissions, approvals, source references, and audit logs are built into the application.

What this means for the business

People can see why a decision was made and who approved it.

05Feedback layer

Feedback improves the system.

Corrections, approvals, rejections, and edge cases become signals for improvement.

What this means for the business

The system becomes more useful as the team uses it.
