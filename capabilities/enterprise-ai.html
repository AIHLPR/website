Applications

Where enterprise AI lives.

The shape of an engagement varies, but the underlying engineering is consistent. Below are the kinds of work we take on most often.

01Vision

Asset operations & classification.

A vision model classifies marketing assets, products, or documents. A workflow layer routes them for human review. The system handles the volume; people handle the judgement.

Stack · Vision model · fine-tuned · routing · review queue

02Knowledge

Document intelligence.

Extract, classify, and search across contracts, policies, SOPs, and prior decisions. The AI answers questions and cites the source so people can verify before acting.

Stack · RAG · vector search · OCR · permissioned retrieval

03Service

Customer service intelligence.

Triage tickets, draft responses, route escalations, monitor sentiment across languages. Agents stay in the loop. Volume scales without proportional headcount.

Stack · LLM · multilingual · sentiment · SLA monitoring

04Research

R&D acceleration.

Mine prior research, prior patents, prior trials. Surface what is relevant, summarise what is decided, flag what is missing. Researchers spend more time researching.

Stack · Retrieval · summarisation · structured extraction

05Decision

Decision support & forecasting.

Predict the next move. Demand, churn, payment timing, inventory pressure, support volume. The AI prepares the recommendation, a person signs it off.

Stack · Forecasting · explainable signals · approval gate

06Operations

Operations intelligence.

Across approvals, finance, procurement, compliance, the AI handles the lookup, the prep, the routing. People keep the decision. Every action recorded.

Stack · Agents · ERP integration · policy enforcement · audit

Human operations

Clear ownership across the chain.

Across the organisation, AI runs under people. Each role carries defined responsibility, a defined escalation, and a defined trace, so accountability stays human even when the work moves through machines.

ROLE · 01

Owner

Business outcome and budget. Approves scope, signs off on production.

Typically VP, department head, or division lead.

Escalates to: steering committee

ROLE · 02

Operator

Runs the workflow day-to-day. Triggers, monitors, intervenes when AI flags an item for human attention.

Embedded in the originating team.

Escalates to: reviewer queue

ROLE · 03

Reviewer

Human-in-loop checkpoint. Approves outbound actions, rejects out-of-scope outputs, attaches reason of record.

SLA-bound. Local language & region.

Escalates to: compliance / legal

ROLE · 04

Auditor

Reads the trail. Verifies that every decision is reachable, every action attributable, every export sealed.

Independent of operating teams.

Reports to: board · regulator
