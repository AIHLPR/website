The problem

Translation was not the hard part. Coordinating market-ready content was.

The client did not only need translated words. Each market needed product content that stayed close to the approved brand language while still feeling local, accurate, and useful for the retailer environment.

A simple translation tool or custom GPT could help produce a first draft, but it would not solve the operational problem around the draft. Product terms still needed to be controlled. Claims had to stay consistent. Market teams needed space to clarify nuance. Brand teams needed visibility before changes went live. Every round of clarification created more communication between central teams, agencies, and local markets.

The risk was not only slow translation. It was brand drift. When local markets were unsatisfied with copy or imagery, they naturally wanted to improve it. But if each market adapted content separately, the brand system became harder to protect. The workflow needed to support localisation without turning every market into a separate creative production line.

The approach

Localise the content while the asset is still editable.

Step 01

Start with approved source copy.

The workflow begins with approved product copy, feature claims, and brand language so every market version starts from the same controlled source.

Source copy · Claim control
Step 02

Apply glossary and brand rules.

Approved terminology, phrase patterns, and brand tone rules guide the first draft so market reviewers are not starting from a generic translation.

Glossary · Brand language
Step 03

Route drafts to market review.

Regional reviewers can check copy while the asset is still in progress, reducing late-stage corrections and keeping local nuance inside the workflow.

Market review · Human approval
Step 04

Prepare retailer-ready outputs.

Final copy can be structured for PDP modules, Amazon content, and campaign asset use, instead of living as disconnected translation files.

Structured export · Retail readiness
