Where it got stuck

Launches were ready. The listings weren’t.

Photography was shot. Copy was written. Launch dates were set. But turning those inputs into Amazon-ready PDP and A+ modules sat as a queue on the digital content team’s desk. Data prep alone — gathering, naming, cross-checking — could take days. Layout wasn’t in-house, so the work was briefed out, with revision rounds and waiting in between. Every week a listing stayed dark was a week of sales the brand didn’t capture — and a week the team felt the backlog grow.

Data prep Layout outsourced Revision loops Backlog growing
What changed

The team kept the decisions. The grind went away.

The workflow took the structural work off the team’s plate — the parts that are repeatable and rule-driven: padding, brand styles, layout per product, desktop and mobile in one pass. What stayed with the team is what should: which photo leads, which callout matters, sign-off before anything ships. Same people. Same brand judgement. No new tooling to learn, no external loop for routine modules.

How we solved the bottlenecks

Each pain point, named, with what changed.

01 Input structure

Inputs arrived in different shapes.

Approved images, product data, and spec sheets were available, but the workflow needed them structured before layout generation could begin.

02 Layout logic

Production decisions were repeated manually.

Image zones, callout placement, spacing, text hierarchy, and module type had to be applied consistently across PDP and A+ formats.

03 Brand constraints

Automation had to stay inside production rules.

The system needed to assemble assets from approved inputs, preserve source imagery, and keep outputs reviewable before export.

04 Review loop

Revisions moved across too many handoffs.

Copy edits, callout changes, and placement fixes needed to happen inside the workflow instead of triggering another production cycle.

05 Production control

Generation needed fixed boundaries.

The app could not behave like open-ended creative generation. It had to assemble assets from approved inputs, keep source imagery intact, apply layout rules, and route every pack through review before export.
