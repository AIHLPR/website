The problem

Marketing assets couldn't keep pace with launches.

A juvenile products manufacturer was launching dozens of SKUs across three markets every quarter. Each launch needed Amazon A+ content, PDP imagery, and localized product copy.

The bottleneck was creative review. Designers built assets one product at a time. Brand managers reviewed everything by hand. Localization happened last, and inconsistencies surfaced in production — wrong colorways, off-brand typography, mistranslated claims.

The team needed a system that scaled volume without scaling headcount, and protected brand consistency without protecting the bottleneck.

The approach

Vision AI for volume. Humans for the decisions that matter.

Step 01

Classify every incoming asset by product, category, and intent.

A vision model trained on the client's catalog recognized SKUs, backgrounds, and composition types automatically — replacing the manual sorting designers were doing.

Vision model · Classification
Step 02

Route assets through a workflow that mirrors how the team actually reviews.

Brand managers, regional leads, and legal got each asset only when their input was needed. No more email threads. No more "is this approved?" Slack messages.

Workflow engine · Role-based routing
Step 03

Compose layouts with a model that understands the brand's visual rules.

Brand-aware composition: the AI proposed layouts that conformed to grid, typography, and color rules from the existing system. Humans adjusted, didn't start from scratch.

Brand model · Composition
Step 04

Localize copy and creative in parallel — not as an afterthought.

Multi-locale translation ran alongside composition, not after it. Regional reviewers got translated drafts when the visual was still editable, catching cultural mismatches before they shipped.

Localization · In-flight QA
What we built

A four-layer asset operations engine.

Designed to be operated by the existing team. No new tools, no new logins — the engine plugged into the asset management system the team already used.

Vision classifier

Tagged every asset by product, category, intent, and composition type. Trained on the client catalog, retrainable as the catalog grew.

Workflow engine

Role-based routing rules built around how the team actually worked. Brand, regional, and legal reviewers each got what they needed.

Brand composer

A composition layer that proposed layouts consistent with grid, typography, and color tokens already defined in the brand system.

Locale layer

In-flight translation paired with regional review — translated drafts arrived while creative was still editable.

Applicable to

The same engine, in adjacent industries.

The pattern — vision AI for classification, workflow for routing, brand model for composition, locale layer for output — generalizes to any operation that produces high-volume creative at brand quality.
