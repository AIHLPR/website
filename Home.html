The stack

Built around your systems, not ours.

Every AI system needs three decisions: which model to use, where the knowledge comes from, and where the system should run. We make those choices around your workflow, data, and security requirements.

Three layers, every time.

01 The intelligence layer

The right model for the job.

We choose the model based on the task, risk level, cost, and accuracy needed. Frontier, open-source, fine-tuned, or switched later when the job changes.

Model choice stays flexible.

02 The knowledge layer

Answers grounded in your data.

We design where the system gets its context from: documents, databases, product data, policies, CRM records, or knowledge bases. The model should not guess when the answer already exists.

Relational, vector, or hybrid retrieval, matched to the data.

03 The deployment layer

Deployed where it makes sense.

The system can run in your cloud, our managed environment, EU-hosted infrastructure, or private deployment depending on security, governance, and integration needs.

Your environment, your cloud, or on-prem where needed.

FAQ

Questions we get asked early.

If yours is not here, the readiness check is the fastest way to answer it.

How do you decide what should use AI and what should not? +
We map the workflow first: where humans stay involved, where decisions happen, and where AI creates measurable value.
Can AI work with our existing systems? +
Yes. Most deployments integrate into the systems already running the business: ERP, CRM, ecommerce, support, documents, and internal workflows.
How long until something usable is live? +
Smaller systems can go live in weeks. Larger deployments move through prototype, rollout, and production hardening phases.
Why build custom systems instead of using off-the-shelf AI tools? +
Generic tools work until workflows become complex. Custom systems fit your approvals, processes, teams, and constraints.
Who owns the IP and the systems we build? +
You do. Workflows, interfaces, logic, and deployment environments stay under your control unless agreed otherwise.
How do you handle GDPR and regional data requirements? +
Systems can be deployed with EU-hosted infrastructure, regional storage boundaries, scoped access, audit trails, and customer-controlled policies.
What about the EU AI Act and enterprise governance? +
We design systems with human review, approval chains, traceability, permissions, and oversight where required.
How do you prevent sensitive company data from leaking into AI systems? +
Deployments can be designed with private infrastructure, scoped access, restricted model permissions, and controlled data handling policies depending on compliance requirements.
